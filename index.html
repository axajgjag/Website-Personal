The BMRT (Barelang Marine Robotics Team) team at Batam State Polytechnic not only focuses on developing underwater robots (ROVs) for competitions such as KRBAI, but is also active in several other competitions such as KKCTBN (National Unmanned Fast Boat Contest) or what is now called KKI (Indonesian Ship Contest). In KKCTBN, they design unmanned ships that can operate autonomously on the water surface, while in KKI, they focus more on ship design and innovation. In addition, the team was also involved in the development of VTOL (Vertical Take-Off and Landing) drone technology, designed for vertical take-off and landing. All these projects demonstrate the BMRT team's capabilities in various aspects of marine and aerial robotics technology, as well as their creativity and technical skills in various fields.

KKI

The Indonesian Ship Contest (KKI) at Batam State Polytechnic is an annual competition that encourages students to design and build small vessels with modern technology, such as automatic navigation and remote control. The focus is on testing participants' technical ability, collaboration, and creativity in building unmanned fast boats, autonomous boats, and eco-friendly vessels. The competition also hones skills in programming, electronic control, and proper material selection.

Discover the exciting world of Vertical Take Off and Landing Quadcopter!

VTOL

The KRTI-VTOL (Indonesian Flying Robot Contest - Vertical Take-Off and Landing) at Batam State Polytechnic is part of the robotics team's efforts to develop unmanned aircraft technology capable of vertical take-off and landing. In this competition, the Polibatam team focuses on design innovation and implementation of efficient VTOL technology that can be used in various missions, such as aerial monitoring or rescue missions. The team combined aeronautical engineering, programming, and robotics skills to deliver a reliable autonomous flight solution.
